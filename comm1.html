1 - Leverage You Will Not Use

This is one of the main reasons for the dispensation of trading accounts.

Many people wrongly think that they can make a living from trading with a capital of less than 100,000 euros.

If this is your objective, then you will have to use the leverage offered by some stock market products: CFDs, futures, SRDs, warrants, turbos ... Except that in case of difficulty on a position, a significant event on an asset on which you are positioned, your loss will be multiplied tenfold.

If you use a trading account of â‚¬10,000, and a lot size of 1 lot per position on FOREX, i.e. invested on 100k, each pip of variation of the pair will correspond to 10 dollars if you are on the Euro US Dollar, i.e. approximately 9 euros at the moment. So a 10 pip change in the opposite direction of your position will result in a loss of approximately 90 euros or 0.9% of your capital.

If you consider the Spread (the difference between the bid and ask price offered by the market or your broker), in just 10 pips your loss will be around 1% of your capital.

Whether on FOREX or any other stock market asset or product, this is a tiny variation compared to the average volatility. You cannot, with such a small variation, put 1% of your capital at risk. In case of several consecutive losing trades, the use of leverage will result in the almost complete loss of your capital in a few weeks or months at the most.

You must reason in terms of the percentage variation of a position in relation to your capital and not in euros. For example, take a savings account, which in 2020 will earn an average of 0.75% interest per year.

Are you sure that risking 1% performance in less than 1 minute is viable in the long term? We recommend using a leverage of 1 to preserve your capital truly over time so that you do not risk one day or another because of a major event (geopolitical disaster, meteorologist, central bank intervention, etc... ) to lose all or part of your capital and give back to the market all the benef its of the efforts and work you have done in Trading for months or years.

One of Trading's basic rules is not to risk losing more than 1% of your capital per trade. Therefore, even in the event of a signif icant event, if your position should be slipped, if there is a price gap, a selloff, a bankruptcy ... you will certainly lose more than 1% , but you will be able to continue trading. Patience, regularity over time and risk management are the guarantee of success.
